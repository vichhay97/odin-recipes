Oyakodon

Description

A classic Japanese soul food featuring tender chicken and onions simmered in a dashi-based sauce, finishied with a layer of silky, half-set eggs over a bowl of steamed rice.

Ingredients (about 2 servings)

The Base

  • 1 chicken thigh.
  • 1/4 onion.
  • 3 eggs.

The Seasoning

  • Salt and pepper to taste.
  • shichimi togarashi (seven spice) to taste.

The Sauce

  • Liquid: 100 to 150ml chicken or dashi stock.
  • Flavorings:
    • 2 tbsp soy sauce.
    • 2 tbsp sake.
    • 2 tbsp mirin.

The Garnish

  • A sprig of Mitsuba or Parsley. Stem separated from leaves and cut into small segments.

Steps

  1. Remove excess skin and sinew from chicken thigh
  2. Salt and Pepper both sides to taste. Optionally: sprinkle with shichimi togarashi for a spicy kick
  3. Cut 1/4 of an onion along the lines into 1-2cm pieces. Not too thin.
  4. Add chicken to cold pan with a little oil and increase heat to medium-high heat. This allows the fat to render more evenly and the skin to crisp up.
  5. Flip chicken and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes once it lifts itself off the pan.
  6. Lower the heat to medium-low and add the onions. Fry until just starting to turn golden and aromatic.
  7. Cut the chicken into bite-sized pieces, skin-side down.
  8. Add the soy sauce to the pan and allow to bubble and thicken.
  9. Add the sake and mirin. Cook off the alcohol. This should take 1-2 minutes.
  10. Finally, add the stock, the chicken pieces and the mitsuba (or parsley) stems. Cook for 3-5 minutes until chicken is no longer raw.
  11. Beat the eggs and add in three stages. Cover between each stage and allow the eggs to set for 30-60s. Turn off the heat for the final stage.
  12. Serve over warm over steamed rice and sprinkle the mitsuba (or parsley) leaves as garnish.
