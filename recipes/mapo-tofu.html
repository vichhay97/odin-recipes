Mapo Tofu

Description

A searing, soul-warming Sichuan classic featuring silken tofu and crispy beef enveloped in a vibrant, numbing 'málà' sauce.

Ingredients (about 2 servings)

The Base

  • Tofu: 1 block (450g) Soft or Medium-Firm.
  • Meat: 100g ground Beef.
  • Aromatics:
    • 2 tbsp minced garlic.
    • 1 bgsps minced ginger.
    • 3-4 scallions (whites for frying, greens for garnish).
  • Oil: 3-4 tbsp neutral oil.

The Seasoning

  • Pixian Doubanjiang: 2 tbsp, finely chopped.
  • Douchi: 1 tbsp fermented black beans, lightly rinsed and chopped.
  • Sichuan Peppercorns: 1 tbsp, toasted in a dry pan then ground into fresh powder.
  • Chili Powder: 1 tsp.

The Sauce & Slurry

  • Liquid: 1 cup chicken stock or water.
  • Flavorings:
    • 1 tsp sugar.
    • 1 tsp dark soy sauce.
    • 1 tbsp Shaoxing Wine.
  • Slurry: 1 tbsp cornstarch dissolved in 2 tbsp water.

Steps

  1. Cut the tofu into 2cm cubes. Simmer in lightly salted water for 1-2 minutes. Drain and set aside
  2. Heat 1tbsp of oil in a wok. Fry the ground meat over medium-high heat until it is completely dry and crispy. It should be golden brown and the fat should run clear. Remove the meat and set aside.
  3. Add the remaining oil to the wok. Over low heat, add the chopped Dobanjian. Stir-fry for 1-2 minutes until the oil turns a vibrant, translucent red. Add the douchi, garlic, ginger, and scallion whites. Fry until fragrant but not burnt.
  4. Add the chili powder, then pour in the stock, wine, soy sauce, and sugar. Bring to a simmer. Gently slide in the tofu cubes and the cooked meat.
  5. Stir your cornstarch slurry. Pour in one-third of the slurry while gently pushing the tofu. Wait 30 seconds, then add the second third. Repeat with the final third.
  6. Turn off the heat. Add a dash of seasame oil or Sichuan chili oil if desired. Plate the dish and immediately sprinkle the fresh-ground Sichuan peppercorn powder and scallion greens on top. The residual heat will bloom the numbing aroma.
